Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was one of the leaders and ideologist of the national liberation movement in India. His philosophy of non-violence Satyagraha had an impact on the national and international movement of supporters of peaceful change.

In India, Mahatma Gandhi reached almost holy status, and around the world he is known as a legendary peacemaker, who gave his life for the independence of the people from the British colonists. In his struggle he used the principles of non-resistance to evil by force. Philosopher urged Hindus and Muslims to come to terms, and considered a wealth is a vice. It was not like the local millionaires who started to organize the assassination of the spiritual leader. He was shot by an assassin before the death Gandhi made it clear that forgives an attacker. Even in the last moments of his life, he followed his principles.
